# Medical Diagnosis using Probabilistic AI

This project combines Probabilistic AI and Bayesian Networks to predict the progression of dementia and Parkinson’s disease using a powerful mix of MRI and clinical data. By uncovering complex relationships between key factors like age, MMSE scores, and MRI metrics, the model offers unique insights into how these diseases evolve and what drives their onset, empowering healthcare professionals to better understand patient trajectories.

By detecting subtle patterns early—before clinical symptoms appear—the model becomes a vital tool for identifying individuals at risk. Early intervention is key, and with this AI-driven approach, clinicians can offer personalized care plans and interventions tailored to individual needs, improving long-term outcomes and slowing disease progression.

Ultimately, this project aims to transform how healthcare professionals make decisions about dementia and Parkinson’s care. With data-backed insights, it helps guide treatment, foster more informed clinical choices, and deliver better patient outcomes, improving the quality of life for those affected by these complex neurodegenerative conditions.
